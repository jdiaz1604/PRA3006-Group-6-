Explore how biodiversity relates to economic power around the globe.

We connect Wikidata biodiversity indicators with macroeconomic metrics so that you can quickly compare continents and countries. Start with large regional totals, then drill down to the nation level to inspect endemic species counts, endangered species, GDP, and population side by side.

What you can do

Our explorer is powered by live SPARQL queries against the QLever Wikidata endpoint and rendered entirely in the browser with D3.

Continent rollups

See continent-wide totals for endemic and endangered endemic species, nominal GDP, and population.

Country drilldowns

After selecting a continent, zoom into any country to read the latest numbers and compare endemic vs. endangered species.

Reliable sourcing

Every metric is sourced from Wikidata statements (population, GDP, endemic species) and cached locally for quick comparisons.

Correlation lab

Dive into scatter plots that relate endangered fractions to GDP and population to spot subtle macroecological patterns.
